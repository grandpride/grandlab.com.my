Frequently Asked Questions

We get a lot of questions about our services, so we have gathered the most common ones here. If yours is not answered below, do not hesitate to get in touch.

A ceramic coating is a liquid polymer that chemically bonds to your paint, forming a hard, glass-like layer. It protects against UV, chemical stains and road grime, makes washing easier, and adds noticeable depth and gloss. Unlike wax, it does not simply sit on the surface and wash away.

It depends on the product and how the car is cared for. Entry-level coatings such as Gyeon Q² Cancoat typically last around a year, while the flagship Mohs and Infinite can last several years with correct maintenance. Every coating we apply is backed by our GrandLab e-Warranty.

PPF is a clear, self-healing urethane film applied over your paint. It absorbs the impact of stone chips, light scratches and road debris, keeping the panel underneath in factory condition. It is available in gloss and matte and is effectively invisible once fitted.

They do different jobs. Coating protects against chemical damage, UV and staining, and makes the car easier to clean. PPF protects against physical impact such as stone chips. Many customers do both — PPF on high-impact areas like the front end, coating over the whole car.
